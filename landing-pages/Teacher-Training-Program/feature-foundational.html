Four Pillars of Learning Theory

Piaget's Constructivism

Children build knowledge through hands-on experiences. Our curriculum moves from concrete to pictorial to abstract — letting every child construct their own understanding at their own pace.

Stages of Development

Vygotsky's ZPD

Learning happens best in the Zone of Proximal Development — with the right scaffolding from teachers and peers. Our trainers learn exactly how to stretch children just beyond their comfort zone.

Scaffolded Learning

Montessori Philosophy

Child-led discovery, sensitive periods, and prepared environments. Teachers learn to set up Montessori-inspired corners, use real materials, and follow the child's natural curiosity.

Child-Led Discovery

Reggio Emilia Approach

Children as capable, creative communicators. Our curriculum draws on Reggio's documentation practices, project-based learning, and the idea that the environment is the "third teacher."
